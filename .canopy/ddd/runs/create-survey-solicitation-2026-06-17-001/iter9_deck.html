/microplans/program/10008/

Maya stands in her programme portfolio, where the Round 6 study she just finished — Attakar as the intervention arm, Gura as the comparison, both approved — sits as a reviewed study group flagged ready to solicit; instead of hunting for a survey firm in a separate tool, the R6 group card offers a call-for-a-firm button right on it as the way to turn that finished plan into an open call.

Maya Scene 2 of 5

Maya mints the call live and lands on its responses page

/solicitations/create/?program_id=10008&source_program_id=10008&source_group_id=4492

On the create form Maya sees her R6 plan already sitting in a Coverage-areas panel that draws the two wards as a map — Attakar in green, Gura in blue — so she confirms the exact coverage at a glance; she writes the survey scope into the description, marks the call Active so firms can apply, and clicks Create Solicitation to mint the open call and land on its responses page.

The logged-in survey firm opens the public solicitation Maya just published, sees the Attakar-and-Gura household survey drawn as a map, ticks the coverage area on the checklist to commit the work its enumerators can staff, and submits its application against the call she minted moments ago.

Maya Scene 4 of 5

/solicitations/${solicitation_id}/responses/?program_id=10008

Back in her workspace Maya opens the responses for the call she minted a moment ago and reads, on the application that arrived, the exact coverage area she designed — R6 Attakar × Gura — so she is comparing a real firm against the real survey, the loop from study plan to applicant closed.

Maya Scene 5 of 5

Maya awards the firm and sees it was notified

/solicitations/response/${response_id}/award/?program_id=10008

With a firm tied to her coverage, Maya awards the applying firm — the screen records the decision and confirms the organization has been notified, so the recruit-review-award loop is real and shipped end-to-end. The screen stays honest about what comes next — standing up the live Connect opportunity her enumerators would actually run is the work ahead, explicitly framed as not-yet-built.
