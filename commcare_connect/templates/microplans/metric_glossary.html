Plan metric definitions

What each column on the plan comparison table means, and how it's computed.

All metrics are computed over the plan's active work areas — excluded areas (those the lead removed during plan review) are reported in their own columns and are not included in the travel / balance / coverage math.

Workload (the input data)

These columns describe the underlying population and building footprint each plan covers — the workload the rest of the metrics measure against.

Population
Estimated number of people living in the plan's source admin boundary. Carried directly from the boundary's population estimate at plan-creation time (top-down, a single number for the area) — NOT a bottom-up sum of per-work-area apportionments. For a plan that spans multiple boundaries, it's the sum of the source boundaries' populations.
Buildings
Total building footprints in the plan's area (Overture Maps, cached per boundary at generation time). For a coverage plan that's the sum across its work areas. For a sampling plan it's the footprint universe the sample was drawn from — not the sampled count (the buildings the survey will actually visit, which is the plan's work-area count). Pop / building divides by this area total so it stays a real per-structure estimate.
Pop / building
Population ÷ Buildings — the implied per-structure population estimate. A sanity check on the input: values that fall outside the realistic range for the country (typically 2-10) are usually a sign that the boundary's population estimate is stale or that the building set includes non-residential structures.

Travel burden (per-worker territory diameter)

Distance a worker would have to cover to reach the farthest points of their assigned territory. Lower = easier to operate.

Longest worker travel
The maximum territory diameter across all workers' territories in this plan, in km. Territory diameter = the longest haversine distance between any two work-area centroids assigned to the same worker. The worker with the largest diameter sets this value.
Avg worker travel
Arithmetic mean of every worker's territory diameter, in km.
Travel spread
Population standard deviation of the per-worker territory diameters, in km. Surfaces unevenness — a plan with one outlier 12 km territory has a higher spread than a plan where every worker walks 5 km.

Workload balance

How evenly the work is distributed across workers. Lower = more balanced.

Worker load gap
(Max − Min) ÷ Target × 100, expressed as a percent. When population data is available, target = total population ÷ number of territories; otherwise target = total buildings ÷ number of territories. A 0% gap means every worker has exactly the same load; 80% means the busiest worker has nearly twice the target.
Worker load spread
Population standard deviation of per-worker workloads (population if available, buildings otherwise). Catches plans where most workers are at target but one is over- or under-loaded.

Coverage + exclusions

Coverage
Active buildings ÷ (active + excluded) buildings × 100, expressed as a percent. A coverage below 100% means some work areas were excluded during plan review.
Excluded areas
Count of work areas the lead removed during plan review (with a reason — water body, school, unsafe, etc.). Not included in the travel / balance / coverage math above.
Excluded buildings
Total buildings inside the excluded work areas. Reported alongside Excluded areas so the reader can see whether the exclusions were small (one school) or large (a residential block).

Structure

Worker territories
Number of distinct workers (or unassigned groups, before assignment) the plan's work areas are partitioned across. Each worker has one territory; all metrics above are computed per-territory.
Source: commcare_connect/microplans/core/plan.py:plan_kpis. Definitions follow Neal Lesh's microplan-quality spec; the territory-diameter computation uses a convex hull for performance on plans with >50 work areas (exact, not sampled).
